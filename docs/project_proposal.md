## 1. Problem statement

Modern diffusion models (Stable Diffusion, DALL·E 3, Midjourney) generate photorealistic images that are effectively indistinguishable from real photographs to the unaided human eye. This has directly enabled fake news imagery, identity misuse, and AI-generated art misrepresented as human-made. The Pakistani digital media ecosystem is not immune — viral "AI photos" of public figures have circulated locally in 2024–2026. Automated, interpretable detection of AI-generated images is therefore a tractable and useful problem.

**Task:** Build a binary classifier that, given an RGB image, predicts `REAL` (camera-captured) or `FAKE` (diffusion-generated), and explains its decision via a visual saliency map.

## 3. Dataset

**CIFAKE** (Bird & Lotfi, IEEE Access 2024) — publicly available on Kaggle.

- 60,000 REAL images from CIFAR-10
- 60,000 FAKE images generated by Stable Diffusion v1.4 prompted with CIFAR-10 class names
- Resolution: 32 × 32 RGB
- Splits: 100 k train (50 k / 50 k balanced) + 20 k test (10 k / 10 k balanced)

Ethical note: dataset is released under CC-BY-4.0 for academic research. We do not redistribute it; users download it from the original Kaggle page.

## 4. Approach

### 4.1 Pre-processing

- **Standard pipeline:** resize → random horizontal flip → mild random translation (±5 %) → ToTensor → channel-wise normalise.
- **Augmentation restraint:** we deliberately *avoid* blur and aggressive downscaling — recent robustness studies (2025) show these transforms destroy the spectral fingerprints that separate REAL from FAKE.
- **FFT channel (our contribution):** compute the 2D FFT of the luminance channel, take the log-magnitude of its centred spectrum, normalise to [0, 1], and append as a 4th tensor channel. The model then learns jointly from pixel and spectral features.

### 4.2 Models

1. **Custom CNN (from scratch).** Three conv-pool blocks (32 → 64 → 128 channels) → global average pooling → two-layer MLP head. Operates at native 32 × 32 resolution. ~0.2 M parameters.
2. **ResNet-50 (transfer learning).** ImageNet-pretrained backbone; inputs up-sampled to 224 × 224; final FC replaced with a 2-way classifier head. When the FFT channel is enabled, the input conv is surgically replaced to accept 4 input channels — the extra channel weights are initialised from the mean of the RGB filters so pretrained features are preserved.

### 4.3 Training

- Optimiser: AdamW (lr 1e-3, weight decay 1e-4)
- Scheduler: cosine annealing
- Loss: cross-entropy
- Early stopping on validation loss (patience 3)
- Train / val / test: validation split = 10 % of CIFAKE-train (seeded); CIFAKE test set held out completely

### 4.4 Evaluation

- Overall accuracy, precision, recall, F1 (per class and macro)
- ROC curve + AUC
- Confusion matrix
- Grad-CAM heatmaps on a curated set of correct and incorrect predictions — shows what the model is actually keying on
